About me

Madhuri Siddula is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at North Carolina A & T State University. Dr. Siddula obtained her doctorate from the Department of Computer Science at Georgia State University in 2020. Her Ph.D. thesis focuses on privacy-preserving user data publication in online social networks. Her research focuses on privacy-preserving machine learning algorithms for Large Language Models (LLMs) and the Internet of Things (IoT). She is a recipient of the NSF EiR award for her work on Smart health technologies. She is currently collaborating with Collins Aerospace to develop machine-learning models for interiors. She is part of the NSF Expand AI team focusing on Interdisciplinary Research, Education, and Workforce Development. She is the coordinator for the undergraduate Cybersecurity certificate at NCAT.

Dr. Siddula is currently working in Privacy-Aware Computing, Large Language Models (LLMs), Smart Health, Smart City, the Internet of Things (IoT), social networks, big data, and data mining.
